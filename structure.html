The Structure of an Atom




The Nucleus

Atoms are made of subatomic particles. An atom has a nucleus in the center with 2 types of subatomic particles which are protons and neutrons. Protons are positively charged and Neutrons are neutrally charged (no charge). This means that the nucleus is positively charged. Protons and neutrons have the same mass and most of an atom's mass is in the nucleus.



The Electrons and their Orbit

Electrons revolve around the nucleus but it is kept together because of the attraction. The number of electrons differ from atom to atom. Electrons are negatively charged. Electrons are 200 times lighter than Protons and Neutrons. Electrons in the outermost shell have the most energy. Electrons in the innermost shells have the least energy.
