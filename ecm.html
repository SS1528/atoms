Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures


Elements

A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means. An element is composed of atoms that have the same atomic number, that is, each atom has the same number of protons in its nucleus as all other atoms of that element.

Compounds

A compound is a pure substance of 2 more more elements which are combined chemically. A compound particle is a single molecule. When elements join a new substance with different properties is created and this is called a compound. Examples are glucose, water, table salt, CO2 etc. When elements join in the compound they do so in a specific ratio such as for water which is 2 hydrogen and 1 oxygen which is why it is H2O. They are chemically bonded in a specific proportion and the combination for compounds is endless.

Mixtures

Mixtures are when elements of compounds are combined but not chemically. This means that the process of making mixtures is quite easy and reversing the process would also be quite simple. Examples of mixtures can be bronze (mixture of copper and tin) or saline water (mixture of salt and water).
